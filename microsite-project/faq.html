Frequently Asked Questions

Why is it called Pea Ridge?
The area gets its name from the wild peas that grow in the area. When the first white settlers came to the area, the wild peas were very abundant and that's why the area is called Pea Ridge.

Do I have to pay an enterence fee to enter this site?
No, this site is free year round. No enterence fee or pass is required.

Can I bring my pets with me?
Pets are welcome at Pea Ridge National Park. However, they must be on a leash no longer than 6 feet and must be restrained at all times. Pets are not allowed in any buildings unless they are service animals.

What is the park's hours of operation?
Pea Ridge National Military Park's battlefield driving tour road is open for visitors to tour 7 days a week from 6:00 AM to sunset.

Is there public wifi?
The only public WiFi that is avaliable at the park is inside the visitor center. The best places in the park to get a good cell signal are outside the visitor center, the East Overlook, or Tour Stop 7.
