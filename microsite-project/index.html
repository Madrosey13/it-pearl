Welcome to a place of history!

Near the border between Arkansas and Missouri, lies a place that saved Missouri from the clutches from the Confederacy. Today, this 4,300-acre battlefield memorial site honors both side's soldiers that fought and died in battle, with over 23,000 soldiers fighting here from March 7-8 in 1862. Read More

Our Park is acknowlegded as one of the best-preserved Civil War battlefields. Pea Ridge National Military Park features a visitor center and museum, horse and hiking trails, a driving tour, the restored battlefields, hiking trails, a portion of the pre-war Old Telegraph/Wire Road, approximately 2.5 miles (4.0 km) of the Trail of Tears as followed by some of the members of the Cherokee Nation, and the restored Elkhorn Tavern, which was the epicenter of much of the battle.

Our Vistitor Center

Inside the Pea Ridge Visitor Center, there is a gift shop and a museum.There is a 28-minute orientation film "Thunder in the Ozarks", produced in 2001. The park's museum was first opened in 1963, but our exhibits in the museum have been completely updated as of 2010.

the visitor center is open 5 days a week, Thursday-Monday, from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. The battlefield driving tour road is open for visitors. However, it is always important to check the weather forecast before visiting the park.
